Pet Feeder - 2017

Management

Score as many points as you can in five minutes by dragging combinations of ingredients to create recipes.

Created in 42 hours with one other artist for Mark Brown's Game Maker's Tool Kit Game Jam. I handeled all programming tasks including the game's mechanics, progression, and score system. Information on the theme is located here. Our design goal was to create a simple mouse only game that is easy to pick up and learn, but still have some complexity after learning the basics. For example, placing ingredients so that they reduce the amount of time between dragging items is essential for getting higher scores, but new players need not concern themselves with it yet. Another design goal was simple and clear UI. For example, cooking timers are represented by "steam" instead of fillable bars.

To score points, you must first grab new ingredients from the window and place them in your pantry, cutting board, or in a pot or pan. Avaliable recipes to complete are randomly chosen from a pool at random. Follow recipes by cutting and combining ingredients, and then putting them in a pot or pan to cook. Make sure the food doesn't burn! When you are done, drag your finished product to the window to score points. You get reduced points for finished products that don't match any of the current recipes. After finishing a correct recipe, the recipe will turn green and you may then tap it to get a new random recipe. The window refreshes all of its contained ingredients every 30 seconds, so make sure to grab the ingredients you want as soon as you can.



Rubber Ducky Stimulator - 2017

Puzzle - Cooperative

Create waves in order to push all the rubber duckies into the bathtub drain.

Created with a team of two artists and two other programmers for Global Game Jam 2017 and the theme "Waves". I helped design and implement the hazard behaviors, controls, and simulated physics. To play, you will need a friend and two controllers. Use the left analog stick to move your cursor and the action button (for a Xbox360 controller, this should be A) to splash. Splashing causes a wave to appear, pushing away other entities. This is your main mode of duck transportation! You and another friend must then work together to try and push every duck into the drain. There are also four different ducks each with their own characterstics, and there are a plethora of other hazards such as urchins, faucets, and... whales? Yes, whales. Try to get through all 20 stages!



Crate Scorer - 2016

Arcade

Launch crates into hoops to score points.

Developed independently in the span of a week. I was given a basic framework with a C# interface to improve upon. There are 30 stages, and your goal is to launch a variety of different crates into hoops in order to score points and earn more time. The game is over once the timer hits 0. Missing crates will deduct time and falling off the center platform will instantly end the game. One feature: you can hold left click and then release to launch crates different distances, depending on how long you charged for. The enviornment also changes throughout the stages.

Snak(Prototype) - 2015

Puzzle

Use turn tile pads to guide Snak towards all the Stars.

This game was created using Allegro and was developed independently in the span of around five days. If you haven't guessed it yet, this game is loosely based off Snake. In order to guide Snak (the snake) towads all the Stars, you need to turn tile pads and have them face the right direction. When you start the game (after adjusting each turn tile pad), Snak will fire off and follow the direction of each turn tile pad it hits. If Snak hits a wall or hits its own tail, you must restart. For this prototype, there are 4 levels, with some levels having optional coins Snak can collect.


Melody Matcher - 2014

Music

Playback randomly generated melodies to score points.

This was a two person college web project that took around a month to complete. I was primarily involved with the backend of this web application, including the basic gameplay functionality, level progression, and point calculation. Our major design goal was to help players with pitch and tone recognition. Earn points by listening to randomly generated melodies and playing them back on a virtual piano. For each melody, you have a time limit where you must correctly play back the melody or else the game ends. After each correctly played melody, the time limit resets. Although there are levels, the game is endless; Melody Matcher gets progressively more difficult after each successfully melody. Points are also dynamically calculated depending on the difficulty of the melody and several other factors such as accuracy and length of time spend completing the melody. There are three difficulties to choose from and selectable instruments.

Celestial - 2017

Strategy Competative

Face off against a friend in a turn based competative game, where you attempt to destroy your opponent's Star.

PC game created during the April 2017 Ludum Dare game jam. Created over a three day period with the theme 'Earth Day'. I worked with two other artists, and three other programmers. I helped design and implement the grid field, controls, and many other gameplay mechanics. I was also responsible for streamlining asset generation. Celestial is a mix between card games like Dominion, and Battleship. Using Dust, which is the game's currency, purchase different planets and place them in your galaxy in order to obtain resources and defend your Stars. Launch meteors and comets to break through your opponent's defense. Both you and your opponent's galaxy will start with a random assortment of asteroids, which you can destroy for resources. Unfortunately, due to the large scope of this project we were unable to finish the game in three days.
